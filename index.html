About

  • 2009-2011

    Aurora's Journey

    The vision for Aurora Art Collective is born, fueled by a passion for art and culture. Initial planning and groundwork begin as we envision a space to celebrate creativity and inspire our community.

  • March 2011

    Foundation Building

    Fundraising efforts kick off as we seek support from patrons, sponsors, and donors who share our vision. Our team grows as we assemble a diverse group of experts and enthusiasts dedicated to bringing our museum to life.

  • December 2015

    Design and Development

    Architects, designers, and curators collaborate to design a museum that reflects our values and aspirations. Plans take shape, and construction begins as we transform our vision into reality.

  • July 2020

    Growth and Expansion

    As Aurora Art Collection establishes itself as a cultural landmark, we continue to evolve and grow. We expand our collections, engage with our community, and collaborate with artists and institutions worldwide to enrich the cultural landscape and inspire future generations.

Spectrum of Serenity

By Arnold Harrison

This piece embodies the soothing beauty of nature with a harmonious blend of colors, inviting viewers to immerse themselves in a moment of tranquil reflection.

Botanical Blown Glass

By Hannah Storn

This collection brings the beauty of nature to life with delicate floral designs crafted through the art of glassblowing.

Tranquil Stone Haven

By Tori Grant

This collection beckons with its serene collection of sculpted stone wonders, each evoking a sense of timeless tranquility and inviting visitors to pause, reflect, and find solace amidst their graceful forms.

Mountain Grace

By Stephen Hall

In this piece, the artist captures the awe-inspiring grandeur of rugged mountain peaks and lush valleys. As sunlight bathes the landscape in a golden glow and shadows dance across the canvas, a sense of peace and serenity envelops the viewer, transporting them to a place of timeless beauty and tranquility.

The School of Athens

By Raphael

Created between 1509 and 1511, this painting adorns the walls of the Vatican's Apostolic Palace. At its heart, Plato and Aristotle converse, symbolizing the clash of idealism and empiricism. Surrounding them are other prominent figures from antiquity, enriching the scene with their contributions to human knowledge. Through detailed craftsmanship, Raphael captures the essence of intellectual exploration and enlightenment.

Blonde Girl

By Ben Lustenhouwer

This portrait painting captures the innocence and charm of a young blonde girl. With delicate brushstrokes and soft hues, the artist portrays her angelic features and gentle demeanor. Her sparkling eyes reflect a sense of wonder and curiosity, while her rosy cheeks convey warmth and vitality.
